Overview

The Home Depot Shrink Detection Platform is a machine learning-assisted capstone project focused on improving self-checkout loss prevention. The platform was designed to identify transaction risk, surface suspicious checkout patterns, and support store teams with clearer intervention recommendations.

The goal was not just to detect possible shrink events, but to connect transaction behavior, risk signals, and operational workflows into one decision-support system. This helped frame machine learning outputs in a way that could be useful for real store operations.

This project gave me experience working at the intersection of software engineering, applied machine learning, product thinking, and business impact. It pushed me to think about how technical systems can support real users, not just produce predictions.

What I Built

I contributed as a Software Development Engineer on the capstone team, helping design a platform that connected transaction risk signals with loss prevention workflows. My work focused on making model outputs understandable, actionable, and tied to store-level decision making.

I also helped shape how risk information would be presented to users, making sure the system supported practical intervention recommendations instead of only showing raw data or scores.

Key Features

• Machine learning-assisted transaction risk detection
• Self-checkout activity analysis for potential shrink events
• Risk signal organization for suspicious checkout behavior
• Intervention recommendation logic for loss prevention workflows
• Dashboard-style review experience for flagged activity
• Business impact framing connected to store-level operations

Impact

This project helped me understand how machine learning can support real operational decisions, not just produce predictions. I worked on connecting transaction patterns, risk signals, and model outputs to a workflow that could help loss prevention teams identify high-risk self-checkout activity more efficiently. Because the platform was tied to a real business problem, I had to think about accuracy, usability, and how technical recommendations would be interpreted by store teams.

The project also strengthened my experience with applied machine learning, backend logic, and product-focused development. I gained more practice working with PyTorch, organizing transaction data, designing risk-scoring logic, and explaining model outputs in a way that could support faster decision-making.
